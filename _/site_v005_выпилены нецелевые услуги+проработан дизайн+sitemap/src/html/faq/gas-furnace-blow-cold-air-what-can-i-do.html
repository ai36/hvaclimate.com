If your gas boiler is blowing cold air, it could indicate several potential issues. Here are some steps you can take to diagnose and possibly fix the problem:

  1. Check the thermostat: Make sure the thermostat is set to heating mode and the temperature is set higher than the current room temperature.
  2. Verify programming settings: If you have a programmable thermostat, ensure it's programmed correctly for the time of day and desired temperature.
  3. Inspect air filters: Clogged filters can obstruct normal airflow, reducing heating efficiency. If filters are dirty, replace them.
  4. Check vents: Ensure all vents and registers are open and not blocked by furniture or drapes.
  5. Look for airflow blockages: Check external air ducts for any obstructions or clogs that could hinder air circulation.
  6. Restart the system: Sometimes, resetting the boiler can fix a temporary issue. Turn off the boiler's power for a few minutes, then turn it back on.
  7. Inspect the burner flame: If you have access and knowledge, check to see if the boiler's burner flame is lit. A non-lit flame could indicate a gas supply problem or a burner malfunction.
  8. Check the heating system for leaks: Leaks in the heating system can lead to heat loss and insufficient heating.

If the problem persists after these steps or you're unsure about performing them, it's strongly recommended to contact a professional heating technician for service and repair. A technician can accurately diagnose the issue and suggest the appropriate repairs. Attempting to intervene without proper knowledge and skills can be dangerous.
