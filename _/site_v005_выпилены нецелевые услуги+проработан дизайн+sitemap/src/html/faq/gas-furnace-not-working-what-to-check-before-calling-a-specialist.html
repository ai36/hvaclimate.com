Before calling a professional for a non-working gas furnace, here are a few things you can check:

  1. Thermostat Settings: Ensure the thermostat is set to "heat" and the temperature setting is higher than the current room temperature.
  2. Power Supply: Check if the furnace switch is turned on. Also, verify that the circuit breaker hasn’t tripped or a fuse hasn’t blown.
  3. Furnace Filter: A dirty filter can restrict airflow and cause the furnace to not operate properly. Check and replace the filter if necessary.
  4. Pilot Light (for older furnaces): If your furnace has a pilot light, make sure it’s lit. If it’s out, follow the manufacturer's instructions to relight it.
  5. Gas Supply: Verify that the gas valve to the furnace is fully open. Also, check if other gas appliances in the house are working to rule out a gas supply issue.
  6. Air Vents and Returns: Make sure all air vents and returns are open and not obstructed to ensure proper airflow.
  7. Furnace Door: Ensure the furnace door is properly closed. Some furnaces have a safety switch that prevents operation if the door is open.

If you have checked these items and the furnace still does not work, it may be time to call a professional. Regular maintenance can also prevent many issues from occurring.
