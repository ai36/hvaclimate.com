If your water heater is leaking, it can cause serious issues and requires prompt action. Here's what you should do:

  1. Turn off the water supply: Immediately shut off the water supply to the water heater. This will help prevent further damage and reduce the risk of flooding.
  2. Turn off the electricity or gas: If your water heater operates on electricity, switch it off using the breaker or main switch in your electrical panel. If it's a gas water heater, turn off the gas supply to it.
  3. Call professionals: Don't attempt to repair the water heater yourself, especially if you don't have experience. Call licensed water heater repair specialists to assess the situation and take necessary actions.
  4. Temporarily patch the leak: If the leak is coming from pipes or connections, you can try temporarily stopping it using special pipe-sealing materials (such as adhesive or repair tape). However, remember that this is only a temporary solution, and qualified repair is required.

Remember that a leaking water heater poses a serious risk to your home and safety. Follow the steps above to minimize damage and ensure the safety of your home and family.
