The lifespan of a heat pump can vary depending on factors such as usage, maintenance, and quality of installation. On average, a well-maintained heat pump can last between 10 to 15 years. As for maintenance, it's generally recommended to have your heat pump serviced annually to ensure optimal performance and efficiency. Regular maintenance includes tasks such as cleaning the filters, checking refrigerant levels, inspecting electrical components, and ensuring proper airflow. Regular servicing can help extend the lifespan of your heat pump and prevent costly repairs down the line.
