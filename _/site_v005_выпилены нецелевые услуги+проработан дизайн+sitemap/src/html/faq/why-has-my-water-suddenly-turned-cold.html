If the water suddenly turns cold, it could be due to several reasons:

  1. Faulty heating element: If the heating element in the water heater is malfunctioning, the water may stop heating up.
  2. Thermostat issues: A defective thermostat can prevent the water from heating up to the desired temperature.
  3. Accumulation of deposits: Accumulated deposits or scale in the water heater can impede efficient water heating.
  4. Power supply issues: Lack of electricity or gas supply problems can cause the water heater to stop functioning.
  5. Hot water leakage: If there's a leak in the hot water system, it can lead to reduced pressure and, consequently, less hot water reaching the home.

These are just a few possible reasons. If the water turns cold, it's recommended to consult professionals for diagnosis and resolution of the issue.
